Modeling complex systems · Power grids · Ideas

I’m interested in how complex systems behave — and how much of them we can actually understand.

My work focuses on system identification and power system dynamics, with an emphasis on nonlinear behavior in generators and large-scale energy systems.

Alongside research, I write as a way of thinking — about models, uncertainty, and the limits of what we can capture mathematically.

Research

System Identification of Swing Dynamics
Estimating inertia and damping in nonlinear power system models using realistic disturbance data.
Nonlinear Modeling in Power Systems
Investigating where linear approximations fail and how to model strongly nonlinear dynamics.

Projects

Hydropower System Modeling
Modeling turbine and generator behavior for stability and control analysis.
System Identification Experiments
Parameter estimation from noisy measurement data in dynamic systems.

Writing

I write around questions like:

  • What does it mean to model reality?
  • How much of a system can we identify?
  • Where do models stop being useful?
